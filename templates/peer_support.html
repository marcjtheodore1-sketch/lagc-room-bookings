🎓 Peer Support Sessions for Autistic University Students

Fridays, around 11:00 to 13:00 (Dates and time may vary – please check available slots on Calendly)

Navigating college and university life can be confusing – join London Autism Group Charity for a peer-informed, 1-to-1 session to talk about your university experience, in a safe space outside your university.

In this session, you'll speak with an autistic person who understands the higher education system. Whether you'd like to talk through specific challenges or just chat about your interests, the peer supporter is here to help you make your university life feel a little easier!

These are online or in-person sessions, and each one lasts 30 minutes. They're free and open to all autistic university students – you don't need a formal diagnosis to join.

💬 Things you may want to talk about

  • Reviewing your reasonable adjustments
  • Accessing all available support from your university
  • Preparing for conversations with tutors, lecturers or admin staff
  • Understanding university expectations
  • Developing essay writing skills (for humanities BA and MA students)

...and much more. These are just suggestions – you can talk about whatever matters to you.

📍 Location

Online or in-person at Room 4.4 "Rose"

Pan Macmillan HQ, 6 Briset Street, London, EC1M 5NR

(Nearest Tube Station: Farringdon)

Note: Sessions are confidential and judgment-free. They're here to offer peer support, not professional or crisis support.
